# Spotify Ads Working Playbook for an Agent

This playbook synthesizes the recurring operational guidance in the source records. It is a decision aid, not a replacement for the individual records or live verification.

## 1. Select the format from the listening state

- Audio covers screenless and in-focus listening moments. Keep a clickable companion asset and CTA where the buying path supports them.
- In-stream video is designed for moments when the app is in focus; current format pages should determine whether sound begins automatically.
- In-feed or opt-in video is less interruptive and depends more heavily on the visual hook because a user may need to choose sound.
- Canvas adds a short visual companion to audio. Treat it as support for the audio message, not a substitute for one.
- Multi-format campaigns can extend reach across listening states, but platform studies and case studies do not guarantee a result for a new campaign.

## 2. Build audio creative around comprehension

- Limit the message to one or two takeaways.
- Introduce the brand early; Spotify's current creative guide associates first-five-second brand mentions with stronger brand metrics.
- Use a three-part structure: brand introduction, benefit/message, then a clear action.
- For a 30-second spot, current guidance commonly points to roughly 60-80 words. Leave room for breathing and sound design.
- Repeat difficult URLs or offer codes and spell ambiguous terms.
- Choose a natural voice and conversational cadence. Avoid voice performance that fights the listener's context.
- Use music and effects intentionally; restrained, non-vocal backing is often recommended when a voiceover carries the message.

## 3. Build video for the device and objective

- Keep the live ad-spec record open while exporting. Older PDF requirements are historical references only.
- Prepare vertical creative for mobile-first delivery and horizontal creative where desktop or cross-device delivery matters.
- Include sound when the selected format requires it, but make the visual idea intelligible before a user opts into sound.
- Use brand identifiers, on-screen text, and a CTA deliberately. Match the creative CTA to the companion button.
- Reuse existing assets only after adapting framing, safe areas, duration, and audio for the Spotify placement.

## 4. Treat podcast creative as its own environment

- Host-read and voice-talent ads solve different problems: host trust and narrative runway versus scalable reach and consistency.
- Write conversationally and avoid unsupported claims. Podcast audiences may be especially sensitive to credibility and tone.
- Put the CTA in the audio because some podcast placements do not provide a clickable companion.
- Use the current podcast specifications before trafficking; older guide chapters include useful creative reasoning but may contain stale limits.

## 5. Design experiments before launching

- A campaign can contain multiple ad sets, and each ad set can contain multiple ads.
- Isolate variables: audience, message, format, CTA, voice, or background track. Changing everything at once prevents attribution.
- Define the decision each test will support and the metric that will answer it.
- Compare against the campaign's own baseline and preserve the exact audience, period, budget, bid, and creative conditions.
- Frequency controls trade repetition against delivery. Very low caps can prevent the campaign from spending its intended budget.

## 6. Read measurement claims carefully

- Distinguish delivery metrics (impressions, reach, frequency, completion) from action metrics (clicks, CTR, streams, conversion).
- Preserve the denominator and attribution window for every rate.
- Music-conversion PDFs in this corpus describe a historical 14-day conversion window. Verify the current product before using that assumption.
- A case study combines strategy, execution, audience, market, spend, and measurement. Report the result as observed in that case, not as proof that one tactic caused it.
- Research claims require their market, sample, method, comparison, and date. Do not strip a percentage from its footnote.

## 7. Resolve conflicts by source type and date

1. Current live ad specs and help-center pages.
2. Current format, targeting, measurement, and pricing pages.
3. Current creative-best-practice pages.
4. Dated research reports and editorial guides.
5. Historical PDFs and case studies.

If two sources disagree, state the conflict and verify the current live workflow before acting.
